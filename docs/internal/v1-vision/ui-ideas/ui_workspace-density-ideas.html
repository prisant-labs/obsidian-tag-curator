Calming the workspace: density ideas

The panel today shows 6 columns and 7 filter chips at once over hundreds of tags - a power-user data grid presented at full strength on first open. These are four directions for making the default feel calm while keeping every advanced control one click away. Every mock here also moves the filter chips to their own row below the search, as requested. Click the controls - they work.

The everyday loop needs only a few columns: Tag, Count, and the visibility dot, plus a couple of filters (All / Hidden / Unreviewed). Source, Last used, Rule, and the Frontmatter/Inline/Flagged chips are occasional - so the question across these ideas is how to demote them without losing them.
Idea ALean default + a Columns menu

Show only Tag, Count, Visible by default. A Columns button opens a checklist to bring back Last used, Source, or Rule when you want them - your choice persists. Chips sit on a second row, with the rarely-used ones folded under a More chip. This is the most direct answer to "add show/hide columns."

Idea BOne Simple / Detailed switch

No per-column fiddling - a single Simple | Detailed toggle. Simple shows 3 columns and 3 chips for the everyday "hide the noise" pass; Detailed reveals the full grid for power triage. One decision instead of six. Try the toggle.

Idea CCalm list + expand-on-demand

The calmest default: each row is just tag, count, and a status dot. Everything else - last used, source, first seen, the affecting rule, and the per-tag Always show / Always hide pins - lives in an inline panel that opens when you click a row. Detail is never gone, just folded. Click a chevron.

Idea DTriage-first: lead with the job, not the grid

Reframe the surface around what you came to do. Instead of a flat 247-row dump, open on a few collapsible buckets - Needs review, Hidden by rules, Healthy - each with a one-click bulk action. The full table is still a click away ("View all as table"), but the front door is the decision, not the database.
